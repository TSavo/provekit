## Findings

  1. The lifter ran end-to-end against real source. CID is byte-deterministic
     across runs (default seed [0x42; 32]). 13 contract mementos minted.

  2. The v0 expression whitelist is the dominant skip cause (53/69 = 77%
     of rust-tests skips). The canonicalizer is exactly the kind of
     "compose a constructor, call a function, compare against a literal"
     test idiom the lifter is most narrow against.

  3. The bounded-loop pattern in Layer 2 requires single-statement
     bodies. Three of the canonicalizer's parameterized loops use a
     three-line shape (input -> let -> assert) and skip cleanly with
     a structured warning.

  4. Characterization-conjunction (Layer 2 pattern 3) requires every
     atom to be liftable. In the canonicalizer test corpus, where every
     atom involves an `encode_jcs(&v)` method call, this gate releases
     to Layer 0 and Layer 0 then skips each atom individually.

  5. Spec-level claims (sorted keys, no whitespace, escape sequences,
     integer formatting) are operationally enforced by tests but
     UNREACHABLE by the v0 lifter. Surfacing exact-byte equality of a
     method-call result against a string literal would require either:

       (a) The lifter to whitelist `<callable>(<args>)` on the lhs of
           assert_eq!. Adapter-side change.
       (b) The IR to grow a "byte-equal output of <ctor> on <input>"
           atomic predicate. Kit-side change.

     Both are out of scope here; the gap is the deliverable.

  6. The pre-existing `jcs.invariant.rs` and `hash.invariant.rs` in
     this crate hand-author the same shape of claims the lifter could
     not reach. They are loaded by the `provekit-self-contracts`
     orchestrator (separate crate, separate proof, separate CID). The
     lifter and the orchestrator are independent paths today; this
     experiment establishes that the auto-lift path is reachable but
     much narrower than the hand-author path on this crate.
