**Companion papers:** [paper 13: After Grammars](../../docs/papers/13-after-grammars-programming-languages-as-content-addressed-algebras.md), [paper 16: After Portability](../../docs/papers/16-after-portability-the-universal-address-space.md)

## §0: Why this spec exists

LSP hosts each language as a `LanguageSignatureMemento` and each cross-language translation as a discharged `LanguageMorphismMemento`. The naive reading of that is dangerous. If a language's signature were a flat list of independent operations and the cross-language layer needed one `A:op -> concept:op` morphism per operation, the morphism budget would be `N` languages times `|surface ops|` discharged morphisms, and discovering a translation between any two languages would scale with `|surface ops|` as well. For a real language `|surface ops|` is large: `foreach`, `using`, `??`, `?.`, the `switch` expression, pattern bindings, properties, indexers, comprehensions, `for-else`, `with`, `unless`, postfix `if`, `do-while`, the compound assignment family, and so on. Multiply by every language and the catalog stops being `O(N)` and starts being `O(N times |ops|)`, trending toward `O(N^2)` once anyone wants pairwise sameness.

This spec records why that does not happen. A language's full operation algebra is not flat. Almost all of its operations are *derived*: `csharp:foreach` is `csharp:while` plus the iterator protocol; `csharp:using` is `try`/`finally` plus `Dispose`; `??` is `ite` plus a null check; a `switch` expression is nested `ite`s; a property is a getter/setter method pair; a list comprehension is a fold; `ruby:unless` is `if !`; Python's `for` is sugar over `__iter__`/`__next__`. The surface algebra is *generated over* a small core algebra by a finite set of *expansion rules*, and those expansion rules are equations. Orient them left to right and you have desugaring. The cross-language layer therefore operates on the *core*, never the surface, and the budget is `N` within-language collapses plus `N` core renamings, which is `O(N)`. This spec defines: the surface-as-composition decomposition (§1), the desugaring-equation memento (§2), the `desugar` operation (§3), the homomorphic compression and its morphism-count lemma (§4), `provekit migrate` over the broader op set (§5), the concept-core negotiation (§6), the ties to LSP / AMP / the equational portfolio / Lemma 6 / paper 16 Lemma 2 (§7), what this does and does not claim (§8), and what is already built versus a follow-up (§9).

## §1: A surface algebra is a composition

### §1.1 The decomposition

Let `L:core` be a language's *core algebra*: the operations that are irreducible in `L` (in practice `if` / `while` / `assign` / `call` / `return` / the arithmetic and comparison operations / sequencing, plus whatever else `L` cannot express in terms of the rest). Let `L:surface` be `L`'s full operation algebra as a programmer writes it. Then:

```
L:surface  =  expand  ∘  L:core
```

`L:surface` is the free algebra over `L:core` generated by the surface operations, quotiented by a finite set `E_L` of *desugaring equations*: each desugaring equation has a surface operation term on its left and a core composition term on its right, and the quotient identifies a surface term with its core expansion. `expand` is the homomorphism induced by orienting `E_L` left to right (replace each surface operation occurrence by its right-hand side). This is exactly the "core calculus plus macro-definable extensions" decomposition of every compiler front end and every denotational-semantics treatment (Reynolds' definitional interpreters; Mosses' action semantics; the Wadler / Felleisen syntactic-sugar line; Felleisen's *On the Expressive Power of Programming Languages*, where a surface operation is core-definable exactly when it is a macro). The desugaring equations *are* Felleisen's macros. This spec makes that decomposition content-addressed and federated, the same move the rest of the stack makes.

### §1.2 Desugaring is a homomorphism

A desugaring is a homomorphism of the term algebra: it must preserve each operation contract's `wp` (the weakest-precondition transformer carried in the `operation-contract` per AMP / CCP). Concretely, for a desugaring equation `op(x_1, ..., x_n) = e`, the contract obligation is:

```
wp(op(x_1, ..., x_n), Q)  ≡  wp(e, Q)    for all postconditions Q
```

An equation whose two sides do not agree on `wp` is not a desugaring. It is a different program written with similar syntax. *Supra omnia, rectum*: the substrate refuses to call a `wp`-changing rewrite a "desugaring," because then the core image would not be the same program and every cross-language fact transported through it would be a lie.

### §1.3 What this buys

Because the surface algebra is generated over the core by `E_L`, two things follow. First, the cross-language layer never has to look at the surface: `expand` collapses `L:surface` onto `L:core` before any morphism out of `L` is consulted. Second, by paper 16 Lemma 2 (the alphabet plateau) the core operation alphabet is finite and plateaus for a computational domain, so `E_L` is finite and plateaus too: there are only so many shapes of "this surface form is core composition `e`," and once they are named, later language mints mostly form new core *terms*, not new core *operations*, hence not new macros.

## §2: The desugaring-equation memento

A desugaring-equation memento is an `EquationMemento` (LSP §1.2) with two additive tag fields and one extra obligation. It is not a new memento kind. It is an `EquationMemento` elected into a role.

```
DesugaringEquationMemento ⊆ EquationMemento where:
  - kind              : "equation"   (unchanged)
  - fn_name           : the macro's canonical name (e.g. "csharp:foreach-to-while",
                        "csharp:using-to-try-finally", "csharp:null-coalesce-to-ite",
                        "c11:for-desugar", "ruby:unless-to-if-not")
  - formals           : universally-quantified variables
  - formal_sorts      : their sorts (CIDs of SortMementos)
  - pre               : optional context (a side condition on when the macro fires)
  - post              : the equation `lhs = rhs` where:
                          - lhs : the surface operation applied to formals
                          - rhs : a core composition term over formals
  - role              : "desugaring"          (NEW; elects the equation into the desugar rewriter)
  - direction         : "left-to-right"        (NEW; the lhs is surface, the rhs is core)
  - effects           : empty
```

Same canonicalizer (`provekit-canonicalizer`, JCS), same hash (BLAKE3-512), same CID rules as any `EquationMemento`. The `role` and `direction` fields are part of the canonical bytes, so a desugaring equation and a non-oriented equation with the same `lhs`/`rhs` have different CIDs. That is correct: orientation is semantically load-bearing here. Refining an existing `EquationMemento` into a desugaring-equation memento by adding these tags is therefore a successor mint with `refines = <old CID>` per LSP section 4.4, not an edit in place.

### §2.1 Per-equation discharge obligation

A desugaring-equation memento MUST discharge the `wp`-preservation obligation of §1.2: the two sides of `post` have the same `wp` modulo the renaming that maps the surface operation's formals onto the core composition's variables. This is the *same machinery* as discharging any `EquationMemento`: the Maude backend when the obligation is in `equational_theory` (Equational Portfolio Extension sections 3 and 4), or the prove portfolio (Z3, cvc5, Vampire, Coq) for the equational sub-obligations and the structural cases. A discharged desugaring equation produces a receipt exactly as any equation discharge does. There is nothing new at the per-equation layer.

### §2.2 Per-set discharge obligation

The *set* `E_L` of a language's desugaring equations carries an additional obligation that no single equation carries: read left to right as a term-rewriting system, `E_L` MUST be **terminating** and **confluent**. Only then does `desugar` (§3) compute a *function*: a unique normal form per input. This obligation is a property of `E_L` as a whole, not of any member. It discharges through the **CeTA gate already specified in the Equational Portfolio Extension §6**: emit `E_L` as a TRS in WST format; run a termination prover (AProVE, Wanda, TTT2) to a CPF certificate; run a confluence checker (CSI, or AProVE in confluence mode) to a CPF certificate; verify both with CeTA; accept `E_L` as a `desugar` input set only if both CeTA checks accept. No new gate. The same gate that decides when a Maude `reduce` witness is trustworthy decides when a language's macro set induces an honest `desugar`.

## §3: The `desugar` operation

`desugar_L : Term(L:surface) -> Term(L:core)` rewrites a surface term to its normal form under `E_L` oriented left to right. When `E_L` has cleared the §2.2 gate, `desugar_L` is total and deterministic on `Term(L:surface)` and its image is `Term(L:core)`.

When `E_L` has **not** cleared the §2.2 gate (mutually recursive sugar, a macro whose right-hand side does not reduce, an overlap with no joining), `desugar_L` is not a function: there is no canonical normal form. In that case `desugar_L` **refuses** the program. *Supra omnia, rectum*: a non-confluent or non-terminating rewrite has no canonical core image, so there is no honest answer to "what is the core form of this program," so the substrate refuses rather than picking one rewrite order arbitrarily and pretending. A refusal here is a precise request: it names the subset of `E_L` whose orientation broke the gate. Re-presenting that subset as a confluent set (a critical-pair completion, a different decomposition) is the path to acceptance, not a softer policy.

`desugar_L`'s output CID is `BLAKE3-512(JCS(<normal-form term payload>))` via `provekit-canonicalizer`, the same as any term CID.

## §4: The homomorphic compression

### §4.1 The collapse of the composition

The cross-language transport is the collapse of the §1 composition. Two homomorphisms in series:

```
L:surface  --[ expand_L, the macro quotient ]-->  L:core  --[ renaming_L ]-->  concept:core
```

The first arrow is `expand_L` (equivalently `desugar_L` on terms): a *collapse*, **within one language**, the macro expansion. The second arrow is `renaming_L`: a *renaming* morphism (a `contract-renaming-morphism` per LSP §1.4 with `φ` mapping slot names, sorts, operators, and literals), **across languages**, and it is essentially an *isomorphism* onto the negotiated concept core, because all the irreducible cores are basically the same (`if` / `while` / `assign` / `call` / `return` / arithmetic / comparison / sequencing). Each `renaming_L` is the cheap canonicalizer discharge of LSP: apply `φ`, canonicalize, compare CIDs to the concept-core operation. The `desugar_L`-on-terms map lifts to the term level by the initial-algebra universality (paper 13 Lemma 3), the same lift that already takes a renaming morphism on operations to a transport map on terms.

This composes with, and does not replace, the hub-and-spoke topology of LSP and the cross-language-equivalence explanation. The spoke morphism `<lang>:if -> concept:conditional` is `renaming_L` restricted to the `if` operation; it runs at the **core layer**. The desugaring collapse `expand_L` sits **upstream** of the spoke: surface forms are reduced to core forms before any spoke is consulted. Nothing about hub-and-spoke changes; the broader op set just gains a within-language preprocessing pass.

### §4.2 The morphism-count lemma

> **Lemma D.1 (O(N) cross-language transport over the broader op set).** Let there be `N` languages, each with a desugaring-equation set `E_L` that has cleared the §2.2 gate. Let `B` be the plateau bound on `|E_L|` (the per-language macro count) given by paper 16 Lemma 2. Then the total count of mementos the cross-language transport layer must mint and discharge for the broader op set is at most `N · B` desugaring equations plus `N` core-renaming morphisms, i.e. `O(N)`. It does not scale with `|surface ops|`, and pairwise sameness across any two languages costs no extra mementos: it is composition through the concept core (LSP §6, paper 13 Lemma 2).

*Proof sketch.* The broader op set adds no `A:op -> concept:op` morphism per surface operation. Each surface operation is reached through `expand_L` to the core, then through the single `renaming_L`. `expand_L` is `E_L`, of size at most `B` by paper 16 Lemma 2 (the core operation alphabet plateaus, so the set of "surface form = core composition" rewrites over that alphabet plateaus). `renaming_L` is one morphism per language. Pairwise sameness `A:op ≅ B:op` is `renaming_A` composed with `renaming_B` inverse through the concept core, which mints nothing new (Lemma 2 / §6). Summing: `N · B + N` mementos. `B` is a constant of the domain snapshot, so this is `O(N)`. □

Without the alphabet-plateau dependency (paper 16 Lemma 2) the `O(N)` claim is a hope rather than a lemma: it is the plateau that makes `B` a constant.

## §5: `provekit migrate A → B` over the broader op set

`provekit migrate` (2026-05-02-provekit-migrate-protocol.md) over the broader op set is the pipeline:

```
lift(A-src)
  → desugar_A           (A's macros, oriented left to right, to the A core normal form; refuse if E_A failed the §2.2 gate)
  → renaming_A          (A core → concept core, the cheap canonicalizer morphism)
  → renaming_B⁻¹        (concept core → B core)
  → realize(B)          (B core term realized as B source)
```

The correctness obligation of the transport is satisfied when this pipeline lands a **valid B-core program** with the source-level proofs transported along `renaming_A` and `renaming_B⁻¹` (soundness by paper 13 Lemma 4). A B-core term *is* a valid B program; B's core operations are a subset of B's surface operations.

**Re-sugaring is a cosmetic post-pass, outside the transport's correctness obligation.** Applying `E_B` *backwards* to a core fragment to recover idiomatic B (`foreach`, `using`, the `switch` expression, a comprehension) is a *search*: which combination of B's macros, run in reverse, reproduces this core fragment. It is not a function (a core fragment may match several reverse-macro combinations, or none cleanly), it is not part of the transport, and its failure mode is "the migrated B is less idiomatic," never "the migrated B is wrong." `migrate` MAY run re-sugar search as a presentation step; it MUST NOT make the transport's verdict depend on it.

## §6: The concept-core negotiation

Not every operation is "core" in every language. `unless` is sugar in Ruby (over `if !`); Python has no `unless` at all. C's `for` is arguably core (a three-part loop primitive); Python's `for` is sugar over the `__iter__`/`__next__` iterator protocol. So `concept:core` is a *negotiated minimum*: the operations that are irreducible in essentially every language. An operation that is "core in A but sugar in B" still desugars in A (it has a desugaring equation in `E_A` mapping it onto operations that *are* in `concept:core`), so the cross-language form is always the common core. The size of `E_L` is therefore:

```
|E_L|  =  |L's surface operations|  minus  |the operations L shares with concept:core|
```

That is, one desugaring equation per surface operation that is not itself in the negotiated concept core. Operations that are irreducible in `L` and not in `concept:core` (a construct genuinely unique to `L`) are not desugared; they sit in `L:core` and transport only to languages whose core also has them (§8).

## §7: Ties to the existing substrate

- **LSP §1.2 `EquationMemento`.** A desugaring-equation memento *is* an `EquationMemento` with the additive `role: "desugaring"` and `direction: "left-to-right"` tags and the §2.2 per-set obligation. Same wire shape, same canonicalizer, same CID rules, same per-equation discharge. This spec refines `EquationMemento`; it does not introduce a parallel memento kind.
- **LSP §1.4 / §2 / §6.** `renaming_L` is a `LanguageMorphismMemento` of the renaming variety; its homomorphism obligation discharges via the cheap canonicalizer path (apply `φ`, canonicalize, compare CIDs). Composition of `renaming_A` with `renaming_B⁻¹` through the concept core is LSP §6 / paper 13 Lemma 2 verbatim.
- **AMP / CCP.** The `wp` transformer that §1.2 demands desugaring preserves is the `wp` carried in the `operation-contract` of each operation memento (AMP), and the per-equation `wp`-preservation obligation discharges through the same contract machinery as any contract obligation (CCP composition rules apply when the right-hand side is itself a composition).
- **Equational Portfolio Extension sections 3, 4, and 6.** The per-equation `wp`-preservation obligation discharges through the Maude backend (when it is `equational_theory`) or the prove portfolio. The per-*set* confluence-and-termination obligation discharges through the CeTA gate of section 6 of that spec, unchanged: read `E_L` left to right as a TRS, run a termination prover and a confluence checker to CPF certificates, verify with CeTA. This spec adds no new gate; it routes the macro set through the existing one.
- **Paper 13 Lemma 6 (effect signatures as embedded Lawvere theories).** Lemma 6 is the *same quotient move*. An effect signature is a quotient of a free algebra over a smaller theory by the effect's algebraic equations; a surface language algebra is a quotient of a free algebra over the core theory by the macro equations. Both are "a bigger algebra presented as a free algebra over a smaller one, quotiented by equations." This spec is Lemma 6 applied to the surface-vs-core split rather than the effect-vs-base split. It sits in the universal-algebra / categorical-semantics tradition (Plotkin and Power's algebraic effects; Lawvere theories; Mosses' action semantics; Felleisen's macros; Reynolds' definitional interpreters), made content-addressed and federated.
- **Paper 16 Lemma 2 / §4 (the alphabet plateau).** This is *why* `O(N)` works. The core operation alphabet is finite and plateaus for a domain snapshot, so the set of "surface form = core composition" rewrites over that alphabet is finite and plateaus, so `|E_L|` is bounded by a constant `B`, so the total memento count `N · B + N` is `O(N)`. Lemma D.1 (§4.2) is a corollary of paper 16 Lemma 2.

## §8: What this does and does not claim

- It does **not** claim every language fully desugars onto `concept:core`. A construct that is irreducible in `L` and not shared by the negotiated concept core (a genuinely `L`-specific primitive) does not desugar. It sits in `L:core` and transports only to languages whose core also contains it. The compression collapses the *shared* surface; it does not pretend a non-shared primitive is shared.
- It does **not** claim re-sugaring is sound or total. Re-sugar is search, it is cosmetic, and the transport's correctness does not depend on it (§5).
- It does **not** claim `desugar_L` exists for every `L`. When `E_L` fails the §2.2 gate, `desugar_L` refuses (§3). A language with non-confluent macro definitions is a language whose programs the broader-op-set transport will not move until the macro set is repaired into a confluent presentation.
- It **does** claim that *given* `E_L` cleared the §2.2 gate, the cross-language transport for `L`'s broader op set costs `|E_L| + 1` mementos (the macros plus one core renaming), and the total over `N` languages is `O(N)`, not `O(N · |ops|)` and not `O(N^2)` (§4.2).

## §9: What is already built versus a follow-up

**Already built.**
- The `EquationMemento` machinery: the wire shape, the canonicalizer, the CID rules, and the per-equation discharge path through the prove portfolio and the Maude backend (LSP §1.2, Equational Portfolio Extension).
- The CeTA termination-and-confluence gate that the per-set obligation routes through (Equational Portfolio Extension section 6). It already reads equations left to right as TRS rules and CeTA-checks CPF certificates.
- The seq-monoid laws as the first equation-memento instances: `menagerie/c11-language-signature/specs/eq_seq_assoc.spec.json`, `eq_seq_skip_left.spec.json`, `eq_seq_skip_right.spec.json` (and the same set in the C# and Go signatures).
- **The first desugaring-equation instance already exists:** `menagerie/c11-language-signature/specs/eq_for_desugar.spec.json` is precisely a desugaring equation under this spec. Its `lhs` is `for(init, cond, step, body)`; its `rhs` is `seq(init, while(cond, seq(body, step)))`. It predates this spec; this spec formalizes what it already is. Adding the `role: "desugaring"` and `direction: "left-to-right"` tags to it is the minimal conformance step.

**Follow-up.**
- Minting the actual per-language desugaring-equation sets `E_L` (the `foreach`/`using`/`??`/`switch`-expression/comprehension/`unless`/postfix-`if`/`for`-over-iterator macros for each language signature).
- The `desugar` rewriter itself: the `provekit desugar --lang <L>` (or equivalent) implementation that drives `E_L` left to right to the core normal form and refuses when `E_L` failed the §2.2 gate.
- Running each `E_L` through the §2.2 CeTA gate to certify it induces a function.
- Re-sugar search: the reverse-macro search that recovers B's surface idioms from a core fragment, as a cosmetic post-pass in `provekit migrate`.
- A worked `migrate` exhibit over the broader op set (e.g. a `foreach` loop in C# transported to a `for`-over-iterator in Python via the core), with the transported `.proof`.

## §10: Open questions

- **Macro layering.** Some surface operations desugar onto *other* surface operations, not directly onto the core (a comprehension desugars to a fold, which is itself a `while`). `desugar_L` reaches the core by iterating `E_L` to a fixpoint; the §2.2 confluence-and-termination gate already covers this, but the *catalog presentation* of layered macros (do we mint the one-step rewrite or the closed-form-to-core?) is a convention this draft does not fix. Recommend: mint the one-step rewrite (it is the smaller, more reusable fact); the closed form is `desugar_L`'s output, not a separate memento.
- **Side-conditioned macros.** A macro that fires only under a static condition (an overload-resolution outcome, a nullability flag) carries that condition in `pre`. Whether the `pre` is part of the §2.2 TRS or a guard outside it affects the confluence analysis. Defer to the gate's handling of conditional rewrite rules.
- **Re-sugar canonicality.** Is there a *preferred* re-sugaring (the most idiomatic) and is it computable, or is re-sugar always a best-effort search with a configurable idiom budget? This draft takes the latter; a future revision may identify cases where the former holds.

## §11: Why this matters (the closing principle)

The substrate's first axiom *Supra omnia, rectum* binds it. The broader operation set is the place where a sloppy design would have quietly become `O(N^2)`: one morphism per surface operation per language, pairwise. The honest design notices that the surface algebra is a *composition*, a free algebra over a small core quotiented by the macro equations, and collapses the composition before crossing any language boundary. The cross-language layer then never grows with the operation set; it grows with the number of languages, linearly, and the alphabet plateau makes even the per-language macro count a constant. The same discipline that makes "two `if`s are the same construct" a discharged content-addressed fact makes "this language's hundred surface forms reduce to its eight core forms" a discharged content-addressed fact, and the second is what keeps the first cheap.
